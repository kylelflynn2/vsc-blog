How It Works

Methodology & Algorithm

Six CSV tables from Baseball Reference are joined into one master DataFrame. The top 9 players by Plate Appearances become the starters. Every stat is normalized to a 0–1 scale within that group so no single number dominates. A weighted fitness score is then calculated for each player in each batting spot (1–6). From there, the model solves an assignment problem: it evaluates every possible way to distribute 9 players across 6 spots and picks the one combination where the sum of all individual fitness scores is highest. This guarantees a globally optimal result — not just "put the best hitter at cleanup" but the arrangement that makes the whole lineup as strong as possible together. Spots 7–9 fill with the remaining players sorted by OPS.

Spot 1 — Leadoff

OBP (40%) + Stolen Base % (25%) + Contact/K-rate (20%) + Secondary Average (15%). OBP ≥ .370 is ideal.

Spot 2 — Best Overall Hitter

OBP (35%) + wOBA/rOBA (25%) + OPS+ (20%) + WAR (20%). Most plate appearances with runners on.

Spot 3 — RBI / Contact

OBP (25%) + OPS+ (25%) + SLG (25%) + WAR (25%). Balanced OBP and power with the table set.

Spot 4 — Cleanup

SLG (30%) + HR (30%) + ISO (20%) + RBI (20%). Maximum power to drive in baserunners.

Spot 5 — Secondary Power

SLG (25%) + HR (20%) + RBI (30%) + RC/G (25%). Protection for the cleanup hitter.

Spot 6 — Table Setter

OBP (40%) + OPS (30%) + BA (30%). Best of the bottom third — keeps the lineup turning over.

Real splits, not estimates. Rather than applying a generic "+.022" platoon adjustment (the historical league-average edge), this model pulls each player's actual OBP vs RHP and vs LHP directly from FanGraphs — 2025 and 2026 seasons combined. The difference matters: Ian Happ (switch hitter) shows a .099 gap (.373 vs RHP, .274 vs LHP) because he bats left-handed against righties and right-handed against lefties. Carson Kelly shows a .060 gap (.322 vs RHP, .382 vs LHP). Dansby Swanson shows almost no gap (.299 vs RHP, .285 vs LHP) — a league-average adjustment would have overstated his platoon edge. Each lineup uses the split-specific OBP during scoring so the model naturally favors the right hitter for the right game. All stats are then scaled to the same 0–1 range within the nine starters before final scoring, so no single number dominates.

Sources

Data & References

All stats reflect the 2026 season through early June 2026.

  • FanGraphs — wOBA, wRC+, ISO, WAR, BB%, K%, and all advanced metrics. Primary source for 2026 season-to-date batting statistics.
  • Baseball Reference — Standard batting, value batting (Rbat, oWAR, dWAR), sabermetric batting (RC/G, SecA), win probability (WPA, Clutch, RE24), and batting ratios. Exported as CSV for each table.
  • Lineup optimization based on SABR sabermetric framework: OBP-first ordering, platoon splits, and run-expectancy principles.
